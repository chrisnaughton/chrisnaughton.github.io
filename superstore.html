This project aims to uncover critical insights into sales performance, profit drivers, and customer behavior in a dataset for a fictional Super Store in the early 2010's. It uses the "Global Superstore Dataset" from Kaggle. Please feel free to explore the interactive dashboards below:

View Project Code on GitHub

Dashboard 1: Executive Overview & Business Health

  • This dashboard provides our executive overview: from 2011-2014, we generated $12.6 million in sales and $1.47 million in profit, with a 12% overall gross profit margin.
  • Observe the quarterly sales and profit trends. Despite nearly 100% sales growth over 4 years, our profit margin remained stable, indicating a need to focus on efficiency for proportional profit growth.
  • The segment breakdown shows the Consumer segment drives most sales, followed by Corporate, with Home Office sales less than half of Corporate.

Dashboard 2: Discount Impact & Transactional Loss

  • This dashboard highlights where we're losing money, driven by discounts. See our top loss-leading products like "Tables" and "Machines."
  • Tables are a major loss leader, with negative profit margin worsening from -5.1% (2012) to -12.5% (2014), largely due to excessive 70-80% discounts on items like "Lesro Training Table."
  • Even in profitable categories like Binders, we see profit margin erosion: many individual transactions are highly unprofitable (-50% to -200% margins) due to extreme 70-80% discounts on multiple units, e.g., "GBC DocuBind P400 Electric Binding System." Volume isn't consistently translating to profit.
  • The histogram confirms extreme discounts consistently lead to unprofitable transactions, and the heatmap pinpoints "hotspots" for immediate discount practice review.

Dashboard 3: Geographic Performance & Profitability

  • This dashboard reveals our geographic wins and losses. The bar chart highlights consistent loss-leading countries/regions like the Dominican Republic, Nigeria, Zimbabwe, and Uganda, primarily due to large discounts exceeding margins.
  • The global profit map quickly visualizes profitability worldwide, showing our strongest and weakest markets.
  • Many highly unprofitable individual sales, especially for "Binder" products with 0.7 or 0.8 discounts (e.g., "GBC Ibimaster 500 Manual ProClick Binding System"), originate from specific US regions (Central and South), Nigeria, and Sweden. This confirms widespread discount misuse across geographies.

Dashboard 4: Strategic Opportunities & Overall Trends

  • This dashboard identifies strategic opportunities and overall trends. Our highest performing sub-categories by profit include "Paper" (exceptionally profitable) and "Copiers" (e.g., "Canon imageCLASS 2200 Advanced Copier") and "Phones" within Technology.
  • The pie chart shows our profit vs. loss ratio, highlighting the proportion of items selling profitably. The breakdown of loss-making items by sub-category pinpoints specific attention areas.
  • The sales by month line graph reveals clear seasonal patterns, consistently peaking in Q4 (Oct-Dec) with a Q1 dip, crucial for planning.
  • Our Average Lifetime Profit Per Customer is approximately $922.93. While Consumer sales are high, their AOV is declining. Conversely, Home Office shows strong AOV growth, a key area for targeted engagement.
