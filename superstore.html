Project Overview

Welcome to my Superstore Profit Analysis project! Here, I dive into a dataset from a fictional Super Store in the early 2010s to uncover key insights into sales performance, profit drivers, and customer behavior. Each dashboard below comes with a few bullet points to give you quick explanations and context for what you're seeing. If you're interested in the technical details, you can also find all the SQL queries I used for this analysis on my GitHub repository.

View Project Code on GitHub

Dashboard 1: Executive Overview & Business Health

View Interactive Dashboard 1
  • Executive Overview (2011-2014): $12.6M sales, $1.47M profit, 12% gross profit margin.
  • Sales & Profit Trends: Despite 100% sales growth over 4 years, profit margin remained stable; efficiency improvements are key.
  • Segment Breakdown: Consumer segment drives most sales, followed by Corporate, with Home Office sales being the lowest.

Dashboard 2: Discount Impact & Transactional Loss

View Interactive Dashboard 2
  • Loss-Leading Products: Identify top loss-drivers like "Tables" and "Machines," heavily impacted by discounts.
  • Table Profitability: Tables are a major loss leader; profit margin worsened to -12.5% (2014) due to excessive 70-80% discounts.
  • Profit Erosion in Binders: Even profitable categories like Binders see margin erosion from extreme discounts (-50% to -200% on individual transactions).
  • Discount Hotspots: Histogram and heatmap confirm extreme discounts lead to unprofitability, highlighting areas for immediate review.

Dashboard 3: Geographic Performance & Profitability

View Interactive Dashboard 3
  • Geographic Performance: Bar chart highlights consistent loss-leading regions (Dominican Republic, Nigeria, Zimbabwe, Uganda) due to large discounts.
  • Global Profit Map: Visualizes worldwide profitability, showing strongest and weakest markets at a glance.
  • Discount Misuse: Unprofitable sales (e.g., discounted Binders) originate from specific US regions (Central, South), Nigeria, and Sweden, confirming widespread discount issues.

Dashboard 4: Strategic Opportunities & Overall Trends

View Interactive Dashboard 4
  • Strategic Opportunities: Highest profit sub-categories are Paper (exceptionally profitable), Copiers, and Phones.
  • Profit vs. Loss Ratio: Pie chart shows proportion of profitable items; breakdown highlights specific loss-making areas.
  • Seasonal Sales: Clear seasonal patterns with consistent Q4 peaks (Oct-Dec) and a Q1 dip, crucial for planning.
  • Customer Lifetime Profit: Average is ~$922.93. Consumer AOV declining, while Home Office AOV shows strong growth, indicating a key area for targeted engagement.
